Chapter 4 of 4 · SGF's Role

Someone has to build the first one.

A model is only a model until one Oasis actually exists. The Sustainable Garden Foundation exists to be the institution that gets the first one built — funded, sited, and standing — as proof, not theory.

Funding. SGF is a Canadian non-profit in formation, seeking founding donors, funding partners, and Founding Directors to help establish it under the BC Societies Act and guide it toward federal charitable registration. A share of licensing and sales revenue from SGF's own affiliated inventions is directed back to the Foundation, so its funding base is not donations alone.

Land. The first Oasis sites are being identified on wildfire- and flood-affected land in British Columbia — land the old model wrote off, and the new model treats as the starting point. Acquiring and restoring that land is the Foundation's next concrete milestone.

The first Oasis. Not a pilot to be judged and possibly abandoned — a proof of concept, built to be lived in, and built to seed the next one. Every goal in the previous chapter becomes real, or doesn't, at this single site first.

"The wisest act available to us right now is to build: carefully, joyfully, and together."

This is founder-led, early-stage work — led by Guy J.L. Vallières, a retired engineer and hydrologist with 36 years at Environment and Climate Change Canada, specialising in flood and drought analysis across BC and the Yukon. He is a stroke survivor who presents through an AI voice so his ideas reach you as clearly as they deserve. The Foundation is currently seeking Founding Directors, donors, and community members — whichever of those you are, this is where that begins.
